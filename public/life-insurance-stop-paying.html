What Happens If You Stop
Paying Life Insurance?

Life Insurance

What's actually happening

You missed a life insurance payment — maybe you forgot, maybe money got tight — and now you're wondering whether your coverage just disappeared. It didn't happen instantly. Insurers build in a buffer before a missed payment actually costs you your policy.

That buffer is called a grace period, and what happens during and after it depends on your policy type, how long you've had it, and whether it has any cash value built up. Missing one payment isn't automatically catastrophic — but ignoring it long enough eventually is.

Quick answers

What people ask most

DOES MY LIFE INSURANCE END IMMEDIATELY IF I MISS A PAYMENT?
No, your policy generally stays active during a grace period after a missed payment. Coverage typically only ends if the premium remains unpaid once that grace period runs out.
WHAT DOES "LAPSED" MEAN FOR A LIFE INSURANCE POLICY?
A lapsed policy is one where coverage has ended because the premium wasn't paid before the grace period expired. Once lapsed, the death benefit is generally no longer in effect.
CAN A LAPSED LIFE INSURANCE POLICY BE REINSTATED?
Often, yes — many insurers offer a reinstatement window, but you'll typically need to pay back premiums plus interest and may have to answer new health questions or take a medical exam.
The grace period

What actually happens right after you miss a payment

Most policies include a grace period — commonly 30 to 31 days — starting from your missed due date. During this window, your coverage generally stays active even though the premium hasn't been paid.

During the grace period

Coverage typically remains in force. If you pass away during this window, the death benefit is usually still payable, sometimes minus the overdue premium.

After the grace period

If the premium still isn't paid, the policy typically lapses — meaning coverage ends and the death benefit is no longer active.

⚠️ CHECK YOUR EXACT WINDOW

Grace period length varies by insurer, policy type, and state — some states mandate longer periods for certain products. Your policy contract will state the exact number of days that applies to you.

What happens after a lapse

Term vs. permanent policies

What you lose — and what options remain — depends heavily on whether you hold term or whole life insurance.

  • Term life — coverage simply ends. There's typically no cash value to fall back on, so a lapse means you're uninsured until you apply again.
  • Whole life — if the policy has built up cash value, some insurers automatically apply it to cover missed premiums, delaying or preventing a lapse.
  • Reduced paid-up option — some permanent policies let you convert remaining cash value into a smaller, fully paid policy instead of lapsing entirely.
  • Reinstatement — many insurers allow you to reinstate a lapsed policy within a set window, often several years, but usually require back payments plus interest and new health underwriting.
  • Starting over — if reinstatement isn't available or approved, you'll need to apply for a brand-new policy, likely at a higher premium due to age and any health changes since your original approval.
If you're behind on payments

Steps to take right now

  1. Check your exact grace period deadline. Your policy documents or online account will show how many days you have left.
  2. Contact your insurer directly. Ask whether any cash value can be applied to cover the missed premium.
  3. Ask about a reduced paid-up option if you have a permanent policy and can't keep up with full premiums going forward.
  4. Set up autopay once you're caught up, to avoid this happening again.
  5. If it already lapsed, ask about reinstatement immediately — the requirements typically get stricter the longer you wait.
What to do right now
If you've missed a payment, don't wait — call your insurer today and confirm exactly how many days remain in your grace period. If the policy has already lapsed, ask about reinstatement immediately, since most insurers have a limited window before it's no longer an option.
Frequently asked questions
How long is the grace period before a life insurance policy lapses?
Most life insurance policies have a grace period of 30 to 31 days after a missed premium due date, though some states require longer periods for certain policy types. Check your policy contract for the exact number of days that applies to you.
What happens if I die during the grace period?
If you pass away during the grace period, the policy is generally still considered active and the death benefit is typically still payable, though the insurer may deduct the overdue premium from the payout. Check your specific policy terms, since this can vary by insurer.
Can I get my life insurance policy reinstated after it lapses?
Many insurers allow reinstatement within a set window, often up to three to five years after lapse, but you typically have to pay all missed premiums plus interest and may need to requalify with new health questions or a medical exam. Reinstatement is not guaranteed and depends on your insurer's specific policy.
Does a lapsed life insurance policy affect my credit score?
A lapsed life insurance policy is not typically reported to credit bureaus and does not directly affect your credit score. The main consequence is the loss of coverage itself, not a credit impact.
